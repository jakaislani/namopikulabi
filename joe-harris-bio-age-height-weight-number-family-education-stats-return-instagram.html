Joe Harris Bio; Age, Height, Weight, Number, Family, Education, Stats, Return, Instagram

Joseph Malcolm Harris is a good American basketball player who currently plays for the NBA’s Brooklyn Nets. He attended the University of Virginia before being selected by the Cleveland Cavaliers as the 33rd overall choice in the 2014 NBA Draft.

He was relocated and waived after a year and a half with the Cavaliers due to an injury. The Brooklyn Nets signed him in 2016. Harris is one of only three players in Nets history to hit 200 three-pointers in a single season.

In a great performance against the Hawks, Joe Harris scored 13 points. He started the first game of his career in January against the Philadelphia 76ers and had a career-high 16 points.

However, after LeBron James’ comeback and the addition of Kevin Love, he did play fewer minutes. As a result, he was transferred to the Canton Charge of the NBA Development League in January.

Harris had a fantastic D-League outing, scoring a career-high 26 points versus the Maine Red Claws and 23 points over the Sioux Falls Skyforce. The Cavaliers made it to the NBA Finals during Harris’ debut season, but they were defeated by the Golden State Warriors.

Joe Harris Family

Joe Harris has three sisters: two younger sisters named Jaicee and Mackenzie, and an older sister named Kaiti. Yakima Valley College was where Kaiti played basketball in college. Alice Harris, Harris’ mother, underwent two surgeries after receiving a Stage 3 colorectal cancer diagnosis in 2020.

Joe Harris Education

Joe Harris played all four years for the Mountain Goats at Chelan High, while his father Joe served as the head coach.
Prior to being chosen by the Cleveland Cavaliers as the 33rd overall choice in the 2014 NBA Draft, he played collegiate basketball for the Virginia Cavaliers. He was a member of the squad for one and a half seasons until being injured and forced to be transferred.
