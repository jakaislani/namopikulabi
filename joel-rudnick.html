Biography

Joel Rudnick was born in New York on June 27, 1936. Known for both his impressionist-style American landscape paintings and his sculptures, he received numerous artistic honors, including the Allied Artists of America’s Lindsay Morris Award and the National Sculpture Society’s Mrs. Louis Bennett Prize. He and fellow sculptor Edmonia Lewis were both born in New York. He studied at the Art Students League of New York, the Art School of the Brooklyn Museum, and the National Academy School of Art and Design (also in New York City).
