Who is the smallest F1 driver?

Who is the shortest F1 driver in 2021? Japanese rookie Yuki Tsunoda is now officially the shortest driver on the grid at 1.59m (5' 2”) , with a weight of 54kg (8st 7lbs). He is followed by McLaren's Lando Norris, formerly the shortest driver in F1, who stands at around 1.70m.

Are Formula 1 drivers small?

F1 drivers are so skinny because in order to go as fast as possible, the weight of the car and the driver needs to be as low as possible. There are minimum weights in place, but the teams try to stick as close to that minimum as they can, and so the drivers are usually small and slim.

What is the minimum weight of a F1 driver?

According to the Federation Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA), who oversees Formula 1 and creates the sport rules, the minimum weight of the driver and their seat is 80kg (176lbs). If a driver is below this weight, extra weight in the form of ballasts needs to be added to the cockpit to increase the weight to 80kg.
